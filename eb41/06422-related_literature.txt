Bibliography
Euro-barometer 41.0:  Trade Issues, Blood Donation, AIDS, and Smoking,
March-June 1994

This bibliography includes published and unpublished works that are related 
to ICPSR study 6422. It includes works that are based on primary
or secondary analysis of the data, or which describe or critique those
data or the collection methodology. This list represents all items known
to ICPSR as of 2006-09-20.

If you publish a work or know of other works that are based on these data,
please send the complete citation and name of the ICPSR study used to:
bibliography@icpsr.umich.edu.


1. Blanchflower, David G.; Oswald, Andrew J., "Well-being over time in
Britain and the USA." Journal of Public Economics. in press 2003,.

2. Healy, Kieran, "Embedded altruism: Blood collection regimes and the
European union's donor population." American Journal of Sociology. May
2000, 105, (6), 1633 - 1657.

3. INRA (Europe), "Europeans and blood." Brussels: European Commission, Feb
1995.

4. Inglehart, Ronald, Modernization and Postmodernization: Cultural,
Economic, and Political Change in 43 Societies. Princeton, NJ: Princeton
University Press, 1997.

5. Irwin, Galen, "Second-order or third-rate?  Issues in the campaign for
the elections for the European Parliament 1994." Electoral Studies. Jun
1995, 14, (2), 183 - 199.

6. Kaase, Max, "Interpersonal trust, political trust and non-
institutionalised political participation in Western Europe." West European
Politics. Jul 1999, 22, (3), 1 - 21.

7. Labrie, Normand; Quell, Carsten, "Your Language, My Language or English?
The Potential Language Choice in Communication Among Nationals of the
European Union." World Englishes. 1997, 16, (1), 3 - 26.

8. Mahler, Vincent A.; Taylor, Bruce J.; Wozniak, Jennifer R., "Economics
and Public Support for the European Union: An Analysis at the National,
Regional, and Individual Levels." Polity. Spr 2000, 32, (3), 429 - 453.

9. Theiler, Tobias, "International integration and national beliefs: A
psychological basis for consociationalism as a model of political
unification." Nationalism and Ethnic Politics. Spr 1999, 5, (1), 46 - 81.
